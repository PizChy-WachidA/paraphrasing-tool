Methods for Paraphrasing a Research Article

A research paper is a record that comprises a description of your reasoning, thoughts, and views on a definite topic.

Paraphrasing is crucial because it adds significance and worth to your work. The primary objective for academic paraphrasing is to provide value to your writing while considering the viewpoints and insights of other authors while creating a feeling of significance to your subject. Also, through paraphrasing, you can demonstrate that your article is a significant element of your selected field's interest.

How to paraphrase a research material

For successful paraphrase, study, and comprehend the perspectives of various authors;

  • In the paraphrased text, do not add your insight, individual viewpoint, or thoughts. That is why it is critical to comprehend what the author is saying. If you're using new language, a distinct writing style, or sentence structure, you must retain the original perspectives of the writer presenting your work.
  • Be careful to convert the exact line or section you wish to utilize in your thoughts; don't modify the writer's position when paraphrasing and share the same data the author wants to convey to the audience/reader. Don't merely utilize thesaurus, but highlight what the writer intended the audience to know, even if you aren't articulating it exactly as was in the article.
  • Reverse the statement - For quality research paraphrase, you may also choose to twist the text. You do not have to start where the writer did, and you can continue at other portions of the material or proceed from finish to start or likewise.
  • Providing references - After effectively producing your edition of the book or paragraph, be sure to acknowledge your resources to prevent plagiarism. Plagiarism is the immoral practice of taking another writer's thoughts and ideas (whether intentional or unintentional) without due attribution. If you duplicate someone else's work, make sure to cite the source and put the copied sections in quotes. Copying and replicating other writers' work makes you appear unprofessional and diminishes the relevancy of your article. It is one of the factors why paraphrase is relevant.
  • Change your introduction - Alter the way you describe your resources; for example, you may use phrases like "according to" or "summarises that," or you can include the names of your references in brackets.
  • Avoid misuse of other writers' work. In your work, you can utilize a limited quantity of previous study's content since quoting too much might land you in problems for copyright violation. However, there is no set quantity of letters to use until you are no longer secure attempt to use your idea and recognize when you are overdoing it. If your content is too similar to the actual material, it is noticeable that the new material was derived from the actual one, and your paper appears weak. For your research to be significant, it must stand out on its own. Any paraphrased portion of it should solely exist to assist and clarify the findings.

Pointers for Using the Most Effective Strategies for Paraphrasing

Students who want to acquire efficient paraphrasing ways are fortunate since we have some helpful tips and tactics to save your time. Here are the top thesis paraphrasing helpful hints;

  • Avoid imitating the primary source's vocabulary or sentence construction. To build a new phrase, use similar terms and begin from another portion of the article.
  • Quoting is useful when you have to reproduce sections of the material, but you must restrict your usage of quotations if you want your research to be legible and professional.
  • Using your terms, define the statements. Consider it as conveying to your audience what you learned from the topic. For a more realistic outcome, use your writing style.
  • You don't need to cite or rephrase everything in your research paper. Pick areas with consideration, and choose ones that will add benefits to your work.
  • Finally, always acknowledge your references. Especially whether you're paraphrasing, quoting, or summarising, correctly citing the source can help you prevent plagiarism in your dissertation.

Things to Avoid when Paraphrasing

It is equally critical to detect these faults in paper paraphrasing to prevent them. The below are paraphrasing don'ts that you must eliminate;

  • Do not alter the source's actual intent to meet your needs.
  • When paraphrasing particular words, do not incorporate your viewpoint.
  • Do not exclude material that would give the primary source a completely different interpretation.
  • To avoid plagiarism and properly acknowledge your sources.
  • Don't change some words and send them as your own.
